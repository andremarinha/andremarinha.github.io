excerpt: 'The activism of Portuguese university students has been responsible for significant moments of sociopolitical struggle, under the country’s authori- tarian rule and in democracy. Over the last decades, student organisations and networks have mobilised and contested the implementation of edu- cation policies directly targeting the access to the education system in all its ranks. The cycle of contention held in the first half of 1990s with students contesting the Prova Geral de Acesso and the implementation of tuition fees under the Law 20/92 was referred to as the last sign of the political strength of student activism in Portugal, thirty years after the academic crisis that opposed university students to the country’s author- itarian regime. The activism and protest activity of Portuguese students after the decade of 1990s has become less prominent and bounded to sporadic and ephemeral appearances. Yet, the contemporary trajectory and activity of Portuguese student movements, after the anti-tuition fees struggle in 1990s, is currently lacking from the literature. This study aims to contribute to the overcoming of the latter by grasping the Por- tuguese university students’ contentious responses to the reconfiguration of the university’s role and purpose, propelled by a set of national and European policies, and to austerity, to understand if the Portuguese uni- versity student movement have re-mobilised and contested intra-field and extra-field configurational changes or, instead, demobilised and declined. To identify and discern the trajectory of the Portuguese student move- ment, a database containing 266 protest events for the period between 2003 and 2021 was built, identifying its main actors, claims and targets. As data collected on protest events called by Basic and Secondary and Higher Education students for the period between 2003 and 2021 suggests, albeit in a declining trajectory, the recent activity of student groups has been defined by the extension of its discourse to reach a broader audience, articulating itself with environmental, housing, anti-racist and anti-fascist groups.
